Daily Room Workflow
1
Morning Check — Review Today's Activity
Every morning, open the Rooms page. Look at the room grid to see who is checking out today (these will show as occupied). The Dashboard also shows a "Today's Check-outs" list for easy reference.
✅ Check the Dashboard Alerts panel first — it tells you which rooms need attention today.
2
Guest Check-Out
When a guest is leaving, click their room tile (dark blue). A panel appears on the right showing their name, check-in/out dates, and rate. Use the "Check-out" button to mark the room as available. The room tile will immediately turn green (Available).
✅ After check-out, the room automatically becomes available for new bookings.
3
Walk-in Guest / New Check-In
Click the "+ New Booking" button at the top right. A form will pop up asking for: Guest Name, Phone, Room Number, Check-in Date, Check-out Date, and Rate per night. Fill in the details and click "Add Booking." The room will immediately change colour to Reserved (yellow) or Occupied (dark blue) depending on if the guest is checking in right now or later.
⚠️ Make sure the room you select shows as Available (green) before booking it.
4
Room Service Orders
When a guest in an occupied room orders food, click their room tile, then click "+ Room Service." A popup appears where you can enter the items ordered and the amount. This creates a KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket) linked to that room. The charge can later be added to the guest's final bill.
5
Mark Room for Maintenance
If a room needs repair or cleaning before it can be used again, click the room tile and change its status to "Maintenance." The tile will turn red, clearly indicating to your team that this room is unavailable. Change it back to Available once the work is done.
Occupancy Timeline View
The Rooms page also contains an Occupancy Timeline — a calendar-style view showing all rooms across multiple days. This helps you plan ahead.

1
Booking Source — Where did the booking come from?
The "Source" column tells you where the booking originated. Direct means the guest called or walked in. MMT = MakeMyTrip, Goibibo = Goibibo, and Agency = travel agent. This helps you track which channel brings the most business.
2
Status — confirmed vs checked-in
Confirmed (yellow) means the booking is made but the guest hasn't arrived yet. Checked-in (dark) means the guest is physically present in the hotel. When a confirmed guest arrives, click the green "Check-in" button — this updates the room status to Occupied.
3
View / Send Confirmation
Clicking "View" opens a detail popup showing all booking information including phone number. From here you can also send a confirmation message to the guest.
Complete Booking Lifecycle
1
Create a New Booking
Go to Bookings in the sidebar. Click "+ New Booking." Fill in: Guest Name, Phone Number, select the Room, enter Check-in and Check-out dates, and the nightly rate. Click "Add Booking." The booking is now in Confirmed status.
✅ Pro tip: You can also create a booking directly from the Rooms page by clicking an available room tile.
2
Guest Arrives — Perform Check-In
When the guest arrives, find their booking in the list (look for confirmed status). Click the green "Check-in" button. The status changes to Checked-In and the room tile on the Rooms page turns dark blue (Occupied).
3
During Stay — Room Service Orders
If the guest orders food, go to Rooms, click their room tile, and click "+ Room Service." Enter the food items and amount. This creates a KOT for the kitchen AND records the charge under the guest's stay.
4
Guest Departs — Check-Out
On the departure date, click the room tile and click "Check-out." The room immediately turns green (Available). Any outstanding room service charges should be settled before checkout. The booking will remain in your records for reporting.
⚠️ Always collect payment before clicking Check-out. Once checked out, the room is released for new guests.

Chapter 4 · Restaurant
KOT & Table Management
KOT stands for Kitchen Order Ticket. Every order placed in your restaurant — whether at a table, parcel, or room service — goes through KOT. This is the heart of your restaurant operations.
What is a KOT? A KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket) is a record of an order sent to the kitchen. It tracks what was ordered, for which table/room, the status (Pending → Cooking → Served), and the amount. As the admin, you can see all active KOTs and their current status in real time.

Complete Restaurant Dine-In Workflow
1
Guest Arrives & is Seated
Go to KOT / Tables. Find an available table (dashed grey border) and click it. A panel opens on the right side. Click "Mark Active" or place an order to activate the table. Enter the number of guests (pax) if prompted.
2
Take the Order & Create KOT
With the table selected, click the "KOT" tab in the right panel. Enter the items ordered (e.g. "Paneer Tikka ×2, Dal Makhani ×1") and the amount. Click "Place Order." This creates a KOT and the kitchen sees it immediately. The KOT starts in Pending status.
✅ You can add multiple KOTs to the same table as guests order more items.
3
Kitchen Updates KOT Status
As the kitchen works on the order, the status progresses through three stages. You can update these from the KOT List view by clicking the buttons on each order card.
→ →
4
Generate the Bill
When the guest asks for the bill, click their table tile and switch to the "Bill" tab in the right panel. All orders for that table are listed with their amounts. You can:
  • Set the GST percentage (e.g. 5%)
  • Apply a Discount (in % of total)
  • Enter the customer's phone number
  • Select payment method: Cash, UPI, Card, or Room Bill
⚠️ "Room Bill" option adds the restaurant charge to the hotel guest's room bill — only use this for hotel guests!
5
Checkout & Close Table
After the guest pays, click "Checkout & Close Table." The table immediately becomes available again (dashed grey border). The transaction is recorded in your Reports for the day.

How to Manage Your Menu
1
Add a New Menu Item
Click "+ Add Item." Fill in: Item Name (e.g. "Chicken Biryani"), Category (Starters, Main Course, Breads, Desserts, Beverages), Veg or Non-Veg type, and Price. Click Save. The item is now available to be added to any order.
2
Update a Price
Find the item in the list, click "Edit," change the price, and save. The new price will apply to all new orders immediately.
3
Mark an Item as Unavailable
If an item runs out (e.g. you've run out of Paneer today), click Edit and toggle its status to Unavailable. It will show with a red badge. This prevents your staff from taking orders for something you can't serve. Remember to mark it Available again once restocked.
⚠️ Items marked Unavailable will still appear in the menu list for your records, but should not be ordered until made Available again.

Staff Management Workflow
1
Add a New Staff Member
Click "+ Add Staff." Enter Name, Role (e.g. Waiter, Chef, Receptionist, Housekeeping), Department (Hotel or Restaurant), and monthly Salary. The staff member is now listed and can have attendance tracked from today.
2
Mark Daily Attendance
Go to the Attendance tab every morning. Each staff member has a Present / Absent toggle. Mark each person accordingly. This builds a monthly attendance record which is used in the Payroll calculation.
✅ Make this a daily morning routine — accurate attendance is essential for correct salary calculations.
3
Record a Salary Advance
If an employee requests an advance, click on their card, then click "Advance." Enter the amount and reason (e.g. "Medical"). The advance is recorded and will automatically be deducted from their next salary payment. You can also clear the advance if it is repaid separately.
4
Process Monthly Payroll
At the end of the month, switch to the Payroll tab. You'll see a table showing every staff member, their base salary, any advance deductions, and the final Net Payable amount. Use the date range at the top to view any month. Once you've paid a staff member, mark them as "Paid" — their row will turn green confirming payment.
✅ The Payroll Summary at the bottom shows totals: Total Base Salaries, Total Advances, Net Payable, and how many staff have been paid this month.
Chapter 7 · Operations
Reports & Analytics
The Reports section gives you a detailed financial picture of your business. You can see revenue trends, occupancy rates, restaurant sales, and compare performance across time periods.
What the Reports Page Shows You
📈
Revenue Charts
Bar charts showing daily revenue split between Hotel and Restaurant income. Use these to identify your busiest days and weeks.
🛏
Occupancy Rate
Shows what percentage of your rooms were occupied over a date range. A high occupancy rate means strong hotel performance.
🍴
Restaurant Sales
Breakdown of restaurant revenue by category (Dine-in, Parcel, Room Service). Helps you understand which service type earns more.
⚡
Electricity & Utilities
Track utility consumption and costs over time, helping you identify energy usage patterns and potential savings.
✅ When to check Reports: Review daily at closing to see that day's income. Do a weekly review every Sunday to compare with the previous week. Monthly review on the 1st of each month to track trends and make business decisions.
Chapter 8 · Admin
Settings & Customisation
Settings is where you set up your business once and rarely need to change it again. This includes your brand name, logo, room configuration, table setup, and theme colour.
🏷 Branding
Change your business name, tagline, and logo
🎨 Theme
Choose a colour scheme (Midnight, Indigo, Espresso, Ocean, Rose)
🛏 Rooms Config
Add, edit or remove rooms. Set type, capacity, and nightly rate.
🍽 Tables Config
Add or remove dining tables. Set floor, type, and capacity.
Initial Setup Checklist
1
Set Your Business Name & Logo
Go to Settings → Brand & Logo. Enter your property name (e.g. "The Grand Residency"), tagline (e.g. "Where Every Stay Feels Like Home"), and upload your logo. Click "Save Brand." Your name will appear in the top-left sidebar on all screens.
2
Configure Your Rooms
Go to Settings → Rooms. You'll see a list of all existing rooms. Click "+ Add Room" to add new ones — enter the Room Number, Type (Standard/Deluxe/Suite/Premium), Capacity (number of persons), and Rate per night. Do this for all your rooms. You can also delete rooms you don't use.
✅ Set up all your rooms correctly before you start taking bookings. The room grid on the Rooms page will automatically show all rooms you add here.
3
Configure Your Restaurant Tables
Go to Settings → Tables. Click "+ Add Table" to add each table: enter Table Number (e.g. T1 or A5), Floor (Main Floor, Bar, Patio, Private Dining), Table Type, and Seating Capacity. All tables added here will appear in the KOT / Tables screen.
4
Choose Your Colour Theme
Go to Settings → Theme. Choose from 5 preset colour schemes: Midnight (dark navy + green), Indigo, Espresso (warm brown + amber), Ocean (sky blue), or Rose (pink/red). You can also enter custom hex colour codes if you want your exact brand colours. Click "Save" and the entire app instantly updates to the new colours.

Important Tips to Remember
🔔
Watch the Green Badge on KOT
The number badge next to "KOT / Tables" in the sidebar shows how many pending orders are waiting. If this number is growing, your kitchen needs attention.
💰
Room Bill = Add to Hotel Invoice
When a hotel guest eats in your restaurant and says "add it to my room," always select "Room Bill" as the payment method. This ensures the charge appears on their hotel checkout bill.
📅
Use the Occupancy Timeline for Planning
Before confirming a booking, always check the Occupancy Timeline to see if the room is free for those dates. This prevents double-bookings.
⚠️
Always Enter Phone Numbers
When creating bookings and billing restaurant tables, always enter the customer's phone number. This enables confirmation messages and helps in case of disputes.
