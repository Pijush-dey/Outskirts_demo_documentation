Check-in a Guest

When a guest arrives at the front desk, this is the full process — from finding an available room to confirming their stay in the system. Takes about 2 minutes once you know it.

01
Go to Rooms in the sidebar
Click Rooms in the left menu. You'll see the Book View — a grid of all your rooms colour-coded by status.
Green border = Available Yellow = Reserved Dark = Occupied
02
Pick an available room
Click any room tile with a green border. A panel slides open on the right showing the room details — type, capacity, and rate per night.
💡Use the filter bar at the top to show only Standard, Deluxe, or Suite rooms. You can also filter by number of guests.
03
Fill in guest details
In the right panel, fill in: Guest Full Name, Phone Number, ID Type (Aadhaar/PAN/Passport) and ID Number, Number of Guests, Booking Source (Direct / MMT / Goibibo / Walk-in).
04
Set check-in and check-out dates
Pick the dates using the calendar pickers. The bill preview at the bottom will automatically calculate the total (rate × nights + 12% GST).
💡The bill summary updates live — you can see the exact amount before confirming.
05
Choose payment mode and confirm
Select how the guest will pay — Cash, UPI, Card, or Net Banking. Then click Confirm Check-In. The room turns dark (occupied) immediately.
✅You'll see a green toast message at the top saying "[Guest name] checked into Room [number]!"
⚠️If a guest doesn't have their ID handy, you can still check them in but make sure you update the ID details before they leave. It's a legal requirement.
🏨 Hotel

Check-out a Guest & Generate Bill

When a guest is ready to leave, generate their final bill, collect payment, and free up the room — all in under a minute.

01
Find the guest's room
Go to Rooms. Dark-coloured rooms are occupied. Click the room the guest is leaving.
02
Review the bill summary
The right panel shows the complete stay summary — guest name, check-in/out dates, number of nights, subtotal, and GST. Check it's all correct.
03
Click "Bill & Checkout"
Click the green Bill & Checkout button. A detailed bill popup appears. Here you can adjust the GST percentage and apply any discount if needed.
Set GST % Add discount % Choose payment method
04
Confirm checkout
Click Checkout & Close Room. The room status immediately goes back to Available (green border) and is ready for the next guest.
💡For quick checkout without the full bill (e.g. if payment was done in advance), use Quick Out instead.
SituationWhat to do
Guest wants to extend stayGo to the occupied room → update the check-out date → the bill recalculates automatically
Guest wants an early checkoutUse Bill & Checkout → adjust dates manually → bill recalculates for actual nights stayed
Room needs repairs after checkoutGo to Housekeeping → Assign Task → select that room → mark as "Post-checkout clean" or "Maintenance inspection"
🏨 Hotel

Manage Room Bookings

The Bookings section is your advance reservation register. Use it for guests who've reserved in advance but haven't arrived yet — via phone, OTA platforms like MakeMyTrip, or walk-ins.

01
Create a new booking
Go to Bookings → click + New Booking. Fill in: Guest name, phone, room number, booking source, check-in date, check-out date.
💡The booking status starts as "Confirmed" (yellow). It only becomes "Checked-in" (dark) when the guest actually arrives.
02
Use the Occupancy Timeline to see the bigger picture
At the top of Bookings, there's a visual calendar — the Occupancy Timeline. It shows every room and when it's occupied, confirmed, or free. You can zoom in or out by picking a date range with the two calendar pickers.
💡Use the filter buttons (All Rooms / Standard / Deluxe / Suite) to focus on just one room type. Scroll the timeline left-right — room numbers stay pinned on the left.
03
When a booked guest arrives — confirm their arrival
Find their booking in the list → click View → click Check-in Now. The booking status changes from Confirmed to Checked-in and the room becomes occupied.

Housekeeping

Assign cleaning and preparation tasks to your housekeeping staff, track their progress, and make sure every room is ready for the next guest.

01
Assign a task each morning
Go to Housekeeping → click Assign Task. Select the room, the staff member, the type of task, scheduled time, and priority.
Full clean + restock Turn-down service Pre-arrival prep Post-checkout clean
02
Track progress on the board
Tasks appear on a Kanban board with three columns: Pending, In Progress, and Done. You can see at a glance which rooms are ready and which are still being cleaned.
03
Check the checklist before marking done
Click any task card → click Checklist. Walk through each item (vacuum, towels, toiletries, etc.) and tick them off. The progress bar shows completion percentage.
💡If a staff member finishes a task, they can click Mark Complete. You'll see it move to the Done column instantly.
Priority LevelWhen to use it
🔴 UrgentGuest arriving in less than 1 hour and room isn't ready
🟠 HighGuest checking out today — room needs to be turned around quickly
⚪ NormalRegular daily cleaning for occupied or vacant rooms
🍽 Restaurant

Taking & Managing Orders (KOT)

KOT stands for Kitchen Order Ticket — this is the digital version of the paper slip your kitchen receives. Every order your restaurant takes goes through here, whether it's dine-in, parcel, or room service.

01
Go to KOT in the sidebar
Click KOT / Orders. You'll see a table map on the left (all your restaurant tables) and live orders on the right.
02
Choose the order type
Use the tabs at the top to select the type of order you're entering:
Dine-in — customer at a table Parcel — takeaway / delivery Room Service — hotel room guest
03
Click a table and add the order
For dine-in: click the table on the map → an order panel opens. Type in the items (e.g. "Paneer Tikka ×2, Dal Makhani ×1"), the number of guests, and amount. Click Place Order.
💡For parcel orders, click + New Parcel. For room service, click + Room Order and select the room number.
04
Track order status
Each order shows its current status. Move it forward as the kitchen progresses:
Pending → kitchen hasn't started Preparing → kitchen is cooking Ready → food is up Served → delivered to table
🍽 Restaurant

Closing a Table & Generating a Bill

When customers are done eating and ask for their bill, here's the exact flow from order total to payment collected.

01
Find the table's order in KOT
Click the table from the map or find the order card in the live orders list on the right side of the KOT screen.
02
Open the Bill
Click the order → click Generate Bill. A popup appears showing: all items ordered, subtotal, GST (18%), any discount, and the grand total.
03
Adjust GST and discount if needed
Change the GST percentage (0%, 5%, 12%, 18%) or type in a discount percentage. The total updates instantly — no calculator needed.
04
Enter customer phone and collect payment
Type the customer's phone number. Select how they're paying — Cash, UPI, Card, or Net Banking. Click Confirm & Send.
✅The table is freed up immediately and the bill is recorded in the Billing section for your end-of-day summary.
⚠️Make sure to mark every bill as paid before end of day — unbilled orders will show up as red in the Billing section and won't appear in your revenue reports.

Menu Management & QR Code

Keep your menu up to date in real time. You can add new dishes, update prices, mark items as unavailable (when you run out), and generate QR codes for each table so customers can view the menu on their phones.

01
Add a new dish
Go to Menu → click + Add Item. Enter the dish name, category (Veg / Non-Veg / Beverages / Snacks / Dessert), and price. Click Add.
02
Mark a dish as unavailable
Find the dish in the menu list → click the green toggle switch next to it. It turns grey — the dish will be hidden from the QR menu customers see.
💡Turn it back on when stock is replenished. You don't need to delete and re-add the dish.
03
Generate a QR code for a table
On the right side of the Menu page, you'll see the QR Menu Control panel. Select a table number from the dropdown → the QR code updates. Click Download Now and print it for that table.
💡Customers scan the QR code with their phone camera to see your live menu — no separate app needed.

Stock & Supplies

Track every ingredient and supply item in one place. The app warns you before you run out so you're never caught short during service.

01
Check your stock status daily
Go to Supplies. The four KPI cards at the top show: Total Items, Critical (must reorder now), Low (reorder soon), and OK. The bar chart next to each item shows current stock level visually.
🔴 Critical — reorder today 🟡 Low — reorder this week 🟢 Good — sufficient stock
02
Restock an item
Click Restock next to any item. Type in how much quantity you're adding. Click Save. The stock level updates and the status colour changes automatically.
03
Daily Closing — fill in wastage at end of day
Scroll down to Daily Closing — Wastage Log. For each item, fill in: how much was used today, how much is leftover, and how much was wasted (with a reason — expired, spoiled, etc.). Click Save Daily Closing.
💡Tracking wastage daily helps you understand patterns — are you over-preparing on certain days? This directly saves you money.
👥 Shared

Staff & Daily Attendance

All your employees — hotel and restaurant — are managed in one place. Mark attendance every morning; this feeds directly into the monthly payroll calculation.

01
Mark today's attendance (do this every morning)
Go to Staff → click the Today's Attendance tab. You'll see every staff member with two buttons — ✓ Present and ✗ Absent. Click the appropriate one for each person.
⚠️Attendance must be marked on the same day — you cannot backdate it. Missing a day means it shows as unmarked in the monthly report.
02
Add a new staff member
Click + Add Staff. Fill in: Name, Role, Department (Hotel / Restaurant), Phone, Base Salary, and Date of Joining. Click Save.
💡Department matters — it affects which section they appear in for task assignments (e.g., housekeeping staff appears in the Housekeeping module).
03
View attendance history
Go to the Attendance Log tab. Set the date range using the From and To pickers. A grid shows each staff member with P (Present) and A (Absent) for every working day, plus their attendance percentage and total absences as donut charts.

Monthly Salary & Payroll

At the end of each month, pay your staff through the system so you have a complete record of every payment — including advances and deductions.

01
Record any advance given during the month
If you gave cash advance to a staff member, go to Staff → click their name card → click Record Advance. Enter the amount and reason. This gets deducted automatically from their month-end salary.
02
At month end — pay each person
Go to Staff → Payroll tab. You'll see a table with every employee, their base salary, advance taken, and the net amount payable. After paying in cash/transfer, click Mark Paid against their name.
✅The status changes from "Pending" to "Paid". This is logged in their Salary History permanently.
03
Generate salary slips (optional)
Click Generate Slips at the top of the Payroll tab to print formal salary slips for all staff. Useful for employees who need it for bank or rental purposes.

⚙️ Settings

One-Time Setup

You only need to do these things once when you first start using the app. After that, the app runs with your real data.

01
Set your brand name and logo
Go to Settings → Brand Identity. Type your property name and tagline. Upload your logo (or paste a link to it). Click Save Changes. Your name and logo will appear in the sidebar.
02
Add your rooms
Go to Settings → Room Management → click + Add Room. For each room, enter: Room number, Type (Standard/Deluxe/Suite), Capacity (number of guests), and Rate per night.
💡Add all your rooms in one sitting — 10 rooms takes about 5 minutes. After that, your Rooms page and Occupancy Timeline will show all of them.
03
Add your restaurant tables
Go to Settings → Table Management → click + Add Table. Enter table number, floor (Main Floor / Bar / Patio), type, and seating capacity.
04
Choose your colour palette
Go to Settings → Color Palette. Pick one of the 5 preset themes or use the three colour pickers to choose your own brand colours for Background, Primary (sidebar/buttons), and Accent (call-to-action buttons). Changes apply live across the whole app.
Midnight (default) Indigo Espresso Ocean Rose
05
Add your menu items
Go to Menu → + Add Item. Add every dish you serve with its category and price. You don't have to do them all at once — you can add and edit anytime.
💡Add your top 10 most-ordered dishes first so you can start using the KOT section right away.
06
Add all your staff
Go to Staff → + Add Staff. Add each employee with their name, role, department, phone, and salary. This needs to be done before you can mark attendance or run payroll.
